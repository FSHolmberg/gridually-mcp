## Tool: `aphantasia_learning_guide`

Add/update these topics with citations:

### Spatial Memory Is Intact
- Bainbridge et al. (2021), *Cortex* 135: aphantasics show deficits in object memory but spatial accuracy is identical to controls, with fewer errors.
- Dawes et al. (2020), *Scientific Reports* 10: across all sensory domains, spatial imagery was the only form with no significant difference from controls.
- Megla & Bainbridge (2025), *Cognition* 254: object memory degrades over time but spatial memory remains stable.

### Non-Visual Spatial Strategies Work
- Monzel et al. (2024), *Cognition* 251: aphantasics prefer non-visual spatial strategies over verbal. These are equally effective as visual strategies. Third category beyond "visual vs verbal."
- Keogh, Wicken & Pearson (2021), *Cortex* 143: working memory capacity and accuracy equivalent to controls using spatial encoding and verbal labeling.

### Compensatory Strategies
- Taylor & Laming (2025), *Frontiers in Psychology* 16: aphantasic college students achieve HIGHER grades. Use externalization, list-making, anchoring.
- Hinwar & Petkov (2025), *New Ideas in Psychology*: three strategies — Semantic Reliance, Condensation of Inner Speech, External Recoding.

### Subtypes
- Blazhenkova & Kozhevnikov (2022), *Consciousness and Cognition* 101: 3.1% have object aphantasia (most common), 3.5% have spatial aphantasia (rarer). Most self-identified aphantasics retain spatial imagery.
- Dance et al. (2022), *Frontiers in Psychology*: two subgroups — "spatialisers" and "verbalisers."

### Prevalence
- Dance et al. (2021), *Consciousness and Cognition* 97: ~4% prevalence = ~320M globally. No gender bias.
- Chee et al. (2024), *Frontiers in Psychology* 15: confirmed across all cultures tested.
- Up to ~9% self-identify as low-imagery (Dance et al., 2023).

### Mental Rotation
- Kay, Keogh & Pearson (2024), *Consciousness and Cognition* 120: aphantasics are slower but MORE ACCURATE on mental rotation. Use analytic strategies.

### How Gridually Helps
- Fixed grid positions = externalized spatial encoding
- Doesn't require visualization — positions encode automatically
- Combines spaced repetition (WHEN to review) with spatial memory (HOW to store)
- Based on Method of Loci principle: Caplan et al. (2019) showed Method of Loci effectiveness is NOT about imagined navigation — it's about placing items at locations

## Tool: `explain_spatial_memory`

Update the "scientific" depth level with:

### The Two-Stream Theory
- Farah et al. (1988), *Cognitive Psychology* 20: visual (object) and spatial imagery are neurologically dissociable. Separate "what" (ventral) and "where" (dorsal) processing streams.
- Tresch et al. (1993), *Neuropsychologia* 31: double dissociation confirmed — spatial interference only affects spatial memory, object interference only affects object memory.

### Why Grids Work
- Spatial encoding doesn't require visual imagery — it uses the dorsal "where" stream
- Bainbridge (2021): aphantasics' spatial accuracy equals controls
- Monzel (2024): non-visual spatial strategies are as effective as visual ones
- Caplan (2019): Method of Loci works because of PLACE, not PICTURES
- Megla & Bainbridge (2025): spatial memory is more durable over time than object memory

### The Method of Loci Connection
- 2,500-year-old technique (Greeks, Romans, medieval scholars)
- Twomey & Kroneisen (2021) meta-analysis: medium effect size across 13 RCTs
- Gridually = externalized, simplified Method of Loci that doesn't require imagination
